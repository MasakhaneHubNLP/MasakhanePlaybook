# Data Collection, Curation, and Governance

Learn how to collect, curate, and manage datasets responsibly, ensuring quality, diversity, and compliance with legal and ethical standards.

## Data Sources

Different types of data sources can be used to build datasets:

- **Web data** – publicly available online content  
- **Curated corpora** – existing datasets and benchmark collections  
- **Crowdsourcing** – data collected from distributed contributors  
- **Institutional data** – data from organizations, research labs, or archives  

## Sampling Strategies

Effective sampling ensures representative and balanced datasets:

- **Random sampling** – unbiased selection of data points  
- **Stratified sampling** – preserving distribution across categories  
- **Domain-balanced sampling** – ensuring coverage across topics or domains  

## Ensuring Diversity

Diversity is critical for building inclusive datasets:

- **Dialects** – variation across regions and language varieties  
- **Demographics** – representation across age, gender, and communities  
- **Contexts** – different use cases, domains, and communication styles  

## Legal Considerations

Ensure compliance with legal requirements:

- **Consent** – obtaining permission from data contributors  
- **Privacy** – protecting personal and sensitive information  
- **Copyright** – respecting ownership and licensing restrictions  

## Data Cleaning and Preprocessing

Prepare raw data for use in modeling and analysis:

- **Deduplication** – removing duplicate entries  
- **Normalization** – standardizing formats and text  
- **Filtering** – removing irrelevant or low-quality data  
- **Language detection** – identifying and verifying language labels  
- **Formatting** – ensuring consistent structure  
- **Noise and toxicity handling** – managing harmful or noisy content  
- **Missing or corrupted data handling** – addressing incomplete or broken data  

## Data Provenance and Traceability

Maintain transparency and reproducibility:

- **Source tracking** – recording URLs, contributors, or origins  
- **Data lineage** – tracking how data evolves over time  
- **Transformation logs** – documenting preprocessing and changes  

## Ethics, Bias, and Governance

Ensure responsible and fair dataset development:

- **Bias identification and mitigation** – detecting and reducing biases  
- **PII detection and removal** – identifying and removing personal data  
- **Anonymization strategies** – protecting identities  
- **Sensitive content handling** – managing harmful or private information  
- **Fair representation** – ensuring balanced inclusion across groups  
- **Risk documentation** – recording potential harms and limitations  
- **Transparency practices** – clear documentation and reporting  

## Cost and Resource Planning

Plan resources effectively for dataset creation:

- **Budgeting annotation costs** – estimating financial requirements  
- **Time estimation** – planning timelines for data collection and annotation  
- **Scaling strategy** – expanding datasets efficiently while maintaining quality
