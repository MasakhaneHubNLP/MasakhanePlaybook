# Recording Equipment and Setup

Essential guidance for setting up professional voice recording for African language data collection.

## Equipment Recommendations

### Microphones
**USB Microphones (Recommended for Beginners)**
- Audio-Technica ATR2100x-USB: Excellent for interviews and single speakers
- Blue Yeti: Good for small group recordings
- Samson Go Mic: Portable option for fieldwork

**XLR Microphones (Professional Setup)**
- Shure SM58: Industry standard for voice recording
- Audio-Technica AT2020: Excellent condenser microphone
- Rode PodMic: Designed specifically for voice recording

### Audio Interfaces
For XLR microphones, you'll need an audio interface:
- Focusrite Scarlett Solo: Single input for individual speakers
- Behringer U-Phoria UM2: Budget-friendly 2-input interface
- Zoom PodTrak P4: Designed for podcast/interview recording

### Recording Devices
**Portable Recorders** (for fieldwork):
- Zoom H1n: Compact and easy to use
- Tascam DR-40X: Professional features in portable format
- Zoom H5: Modular system with interchangeable microphones

### Essential Accessories
- **Headphones**: Sony MDR-7506 or Audio-Technica ATH-M40x
- **Pop filters**: Reduces plosive sounds (p, b, t, k)
- **Windscreens**: Foam covers for microphones
- **Boom arms**: Adjustable microphone stands
- **Acoustic treatment**: Blankets, foam panels, or portable vocal booths

## Recording Environment

### Choosing a Location
**Ideal Characteristics:**
- Quiet background (minimal traffic, air conditioning, etc.)
- Low reverberation (avoid large empty rooms)
- Consistent acoustics throughout recording sessions
- Comfortable for speakers (temperature, seating, lighting)

**Field Recording Considerations:**
- Scout locations in advance
- Test recordings at different times of day
- Have backup locations prepared
- Consider cultural appropriateness of recording spaces

### Acoustic Treatment
**Simple Solutions:**
- Record in smaller rooms with carpet and furniture
- Hang heavy blankets or curtains around recording area
- Use pillows and cushions to absorb sound
- Position microphone away from hard surfaces

**Professional Options:**
- Portable acoustic panels
- Reflection filters that attach to microphones
- Pop-up vocal booths for consistent recording conditions

## Recording Settings

### Audio Quality Standards
- **Sample rate**: 48 kHz (professional standard) or 44.1 kHz (CD quality)
- **Bit depth**: 24-bit (recommended) or 16-bit minimum
- **File format**: WAV (uncompressed) for archival, MP3 only for distribution

### Recording Levels
- **Target levels**: Peak levels between -12 dB and -6 dB
- **Avoid clipping**: Never let levels reach 0 dB
- **Consistent levels**: Use audio interface/software to maintain steady input levels
- **Monitor constantly**: Wear headphones during recording to catch issues immediately

## Best Practices

### Before Recording
- Test all equipment and connections
- Record 30 seconds of room tone (silence)
- Set comfortable levels with speaker present
- Ensure all devices have adequate battery/power
- Have backup storage media ready

### During Recording
- Monitor audio levels continuously
- Take notes on any technical issues
- Record in chunks (10-15 minutes) rather than marathon sessions
- Save files frequently and backup immediately
- Mark any segments that need re-recording

### After Recording
- Backup all files immediately to multiple locations
- Listen to recordings for quality issues
- Document any problems or anomalies
- Organize files with consistent naming conventions
- Begin quality assessment process promptly
