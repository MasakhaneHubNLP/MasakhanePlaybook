# Acknowledgements

The Masakhane Playbook is a community effort that builds upon the work, wisdom, and contributions of many individuals and organizations across Africa and beyond.

## Core Contributors

**Masakhane Community**: The grassroots researchers, students, and language advocates who form the heart of this initiative.

**Community Language Speakers**: Native speakers of African languages who generously share their knowledge and time to make these resources possible.

**Partner Institutions**: Universities, research centers, and organizations across Africa that provide expertise, resources, and support.

## Institutional Support

We acknowledge the support of:
- **African universities** that host Masakhane researchers and students
- **International research institutions** that provide funding and collaboration
- **Technology companies** that donate resources and platforms
- **Cultural organizations** that facilitate community connections

## Funding and Resources

This work has been supported by:
- Research grants from various funding agencies
- Individual donations from community supporters
- In-kind contributions of time, expertise, and infrastructure
- Open source tools and platforms provided by the broader community

## Special Recognition

**Language Communities**: We especially thank the many African language communities who have opened their doors, shared their languages, and trusted us to handle their cultural heritage with care and respect.

**Translators and Annotators**: The dedicated individuals who spend countless hours creating high-quality language resources.

**Technical Contributors**: Developers and engineers who build and maintain the tools that make large-scale language work possible.

**Students and Researchers**: The next generation of African NLP practitioners who contribute fresh perspectives and energy.

## Open Source Contributions

This playbook builds upon many open source projects and freely shared resources:
- Documentation frameworks and templates
- Audio processing and annotation tools
- Dataset management and sharing platforms
- Community collaboration tools

## Ongoing Gratitude

The Masakhane Playbook is a living document that continues to grow and improve thanks to ongoing contributions from our community. We are grateful for every suggestion, correction, translation, and addition that makes this resource more useful for African language work.

If you have contributed to this playbook and would like to be acknowledged, please contact the Masakhane team.

## How to Contribute

This playbook is open source and community-driven. You can contribute by:
- Sharing your experiences with African language data collection
- Translating sections into African languages
- Adding examples from your own projects
- Reporting errors or suggesting improvements
- Testing our guidelines and providing feedback

Visit our GitHub repository to learn more about contributing to the Masakhane Playbook.
