Chapter Overviews

Quick summaries of each chapter to help you navigate the Masakhane Playbook effectively.

Chapter 1: Understanding African Language Data​

What you'll learn: Foundation concepts about African languages, types of language data, and unique challenges in the African context. Key topics: Language diversity, low-resource challenges, orthographic variations, dialectal considerations. Who should read: Everyone - this provides essential background for all subsequent chapters.

Chapter 2: Community Engagement​

What you'll learn: How to build meaningful partnerships with African language speaker communities. Key topics: Ethical engagement principles, finding community partners, consent processes, benefit-sharing. Who should read: Project managers, researchers, anyone initiating data collection projects.

Chapter 3: Voice Recording​

What you'll learn: Technical and practical aspects of collecting high-quality voice data for African languages. Key topics: Equipment selection, recording environments, quality standards, fieldwork considerations. Who should read: Audio engineers, fieldwork teams, technical coordinators.

Chapter 4: Text Annotation​

What you'll learn: Methods for creating structured text datasets including POS tagging, NER, and syntactic annotation. Key topics: Annotation schemes, quality control, inter-annotator agreement, crowdsourcing approaches. Who should read: Linguists, annotation teams, data scientists.

Chapter 5: Speech-Text Alignment​

What you'll learn: Techniques for connecting audio recordings with their text transcriptions. Key topics: Forced alignment, manual alignment, quality assessment, error correction. Who should read: Technical teams working with multimodal data, ASR developers.

Chapter 6: Translation Datasets​

What you'll learn: Creating parallel corpora and translation resources for machine translation. Key topics: Parallel text collection, translation quality, alignment verification, evaluation metrics. Who should read: MT researchers, translators, dataset creators.

Chapter 7: Dataset Sustainability​

What you'll learn: Ensuring your language resources remain accessible and useful over time. Key topics: Documentation standards, repository platforms, versioning, community maintenance. Who should read: Project managers, data librarians, long-term sustainability planners.

How to Use These Overviews​

  • Planning your project: Review relevant chapter overviews to understand scope and requirements
  • Building your team: Identify which team members should focus on which chapters
  • Training preparation: Use overviews to prepare training materials for community partners
  • Progress tracking: Check off completed topics as you work through the playbook
