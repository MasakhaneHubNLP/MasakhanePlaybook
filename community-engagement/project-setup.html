Setting Up a Data Collection Project

Planning and organizing your African language data collection project for success.

Project Planning Fundamentals​

Define Your Goals​

Before beginning data collection, clearly articulate:

  • Primary objectives: What type of language technology do you want to enable?
  • Target applications: Speech recognition, machine translation, text classification, etc.
  • Quality requirements: How much data do you need and at what quality level?
  • Timeline and resources: Realistic assessment of time, budget, and personnel

Choose Your Languages and Dialects​

Consider:

  • Speaker populations: How many native speakers are available?
  • Geographic distribution: Are speakers concentrated or dispersed?
  • Dialectal variations: Which dialects should be included?
  • Existing resources: What data already exists for these languages?

Assess Technical Requirements​

Determine:

  • Data formats: Audio quality, text encoding, metadata standards
  • Storage needs: How much data will you collect and where will it be stored?
  • Processing tools: What software and computational resources are needed?
  • Quality control: How will you validate and clean your data?

Building Your Team​

Essential Roles​

  • Project manager: Coordinates all aspects of the project
  • Community liaison: Maintains relationships with speaker communities
  • Technical lead: Handles data processing and quality assurance
  • Native speakers: Provide linguistic expertise and cultural guidance

Collaborative Partnerships​

  • Academic institutions: Provide research expertise and student resources
  • Community organizations: Offer local knowledge and community access
  • Technology partners: Supply tools, platforms, and technical support
  • Funding organizations: Support project sustainability and growth

Resource Planning​

Budget Considerations​

  • Personnel costs: Salaries, stipends, and contractor fees
  • Equipment expenses: Recording devices, computers, software licenses
  • Community compensation: Fair payment for participant contributions
  • Travel and logistics: Costs for reaching remote communities
  • Data storage and processing: Cloud services and computational resources

Timeline Development​

Create realistic timelines that include:

  • Community engagement phase: Building trust and partnerships
  • Pilot testing: Small-scale trials to refine methods
  • Full data collection: Primary data gathering activities
  • Quality assurance: Data validation and cleaning
  • Documentation and release: Preparing datasets for public use
