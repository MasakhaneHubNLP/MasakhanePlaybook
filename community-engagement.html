Community Engagement

Building meaningful partnerships with African language speaker communities for successful data collection projects.

Why Community Engagement Matters​

Successful African language data collection requires more than technical expertise - it requires genuine partnership with the communities who speak these languages. Community engagement ensures:

  • Cultural appropriateness of data collection methods
  • Higher quality data from native speakers
  • Sustainable projects with local ownership
  • Ethical practices that benefit communities
  • Long-term impact beyond research publications

Principles of Ethical Engagement​

Respect and Recognition​

  • Acknowledge communities as knowledge holders and partners
  • Recognize languages as living cultural heritage
  • Credit community contributions in publications and datasets

Transparency and Consent​

  • Clearly explain project goals and intended uses of data
  • Obtain informed consent from all participants
  • Provide data usage agreements in local languages when possible

Reciprocity and Benefit-sharing​

  • Ensure communities benefit from their contributions
  • Share resources, tools, and results with participating communities
  • Build local capacity for continued language work

Sustainability​

  • Design projects that can continue beyond initial funding
  • Train local community members in data collection techniques
  • Create pathways for ongoing collaboration

Building Community Partnerships​

Finding Community Partners​

  • Connect with local universities and research institutions
  • Partner with cultural organizations and language advocacy groups
  • Work with schools and educational institutions
  • Engage with traditional leadership structures

Initial Outreach​

  • Approach communities with humility and respect
  • Listen to community priorities and concerns
  • Adapt project goals to align with community needs
  • Be patient - trust building takes time

Ongoing Collaboration​

  • Regular communication throughout the project
  • Include community members in project decision-making
  • Provide training and capacity building opportunities
  • Celebrate milestones and achievements together
